Sweet And Wonderful Fruits Of All Time

Apple

  • Apple trees take 4-5 years to produce their first fruit.
  • Apples will ripen six to ten times faster at room temperature than if they were refrigerated.
  • It takes about 36 apples to create one gallon of apple cider.

    Water Melon

  • The first recorded watermelon harvest occurred nearly 5,000 years ago in Egypt.
  • Early explorers used watermelons as canteens.
  • Watermelon’s official name is Citrullus Lanatus of the botanical family Cucurbitaceae.

    Mango

  • Mangoes grow on trees and in tropical regions, including southern Florida and southern California.
  • Mangoes are considered to be the most delicious and most nutritiously beneficial fruit out there.
  • India is the world's biggest producer of mangoes.

    Sour And Tangy Fruits!

    Orange

  • Orange, the fruit, is older than orange, the color!
  • Brazil grows more oranges per year than any other country.
  • Oranges are non-climacteric, meaning they won’t ripen after they’re picked.

    Pine Apple

  • Only one pineapple is produced by a single plant in a single season
  • A pineapple plant can stay alive and continue giving fruits for up to 50 years
  • A pineapple is not a pine or an apple. Its actually a berry.

    • Last updated 3 mins ago

    Strawberry

  • Strawberries are one of the first fruits to ripen in the spring.
  • You might think that an orange has the most vitamin C out of any fruit, but strawberries contains more.
  • According to research, the average American consumes approx 8 pounds of fresh strawberries per year.
